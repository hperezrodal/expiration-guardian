# Brand Manual – Expiration Guardian v1

## 1. Brand Overview

**Product Name:** Expiration Guardian  
**Tagline:** *Never miss what matters.*

Expiration Guardian is a quiet, trustworthy assistant that helps people and businesses track expirations, renewals, and recurring obligations — without stress, clutter, or noise.

Our brand exists to remove cognitive load. We do not nag, scare, or overwhelm. We simply make sure nothing important slips through the cracks.

---

## 2. Brand Mission

To help people stay ahead of deadlines and expirations with calm, reliable automation.

We believe peace of mind is a product feature.

---

## 3. Brand Vision

A world where:
- No document expires unexpectedly
- No renewal is forgotten
- Compliance and personal organization feel effortless

Expiration Guardian aims to become the default “background layer” for expiration awareness.

---

## 4. Brand Values

### 4.1 Reliability
If we alert, it matters. If we are silent, things are under control.

### 4.2 Calm over Urgency
We avoid alarmist language. No panic. No red sirens unless absolutely necessary.

### 4.3 Clarity
Clear dates, clear actions, clear communication.

### 4.4 Respect for Attention
We treat user attention as scarce and valuable.

### 4.5 Ethical Lock-in
Users own their data. If they leave, they keep access.

---

## 5. Brand Personality

Expiration Guardian feels like:
- A calm executive assistant
- A compliance officer who never raises their voice
- A checklist that checks itself

### Personality Traits
- Calm
- Precise
- Reassuring
- Professional
- Slightly invisible

### What We Are NOT
- Loud
- Playful
- Salesy
- Cute
- Overly casual

---

## 6. Tone of Voice

### General Tone
- Professional
- Neutral
- Reassuring
- Concise

### Writing Guidelines
- Short sentences
- Clear verbs
- No emojis in product UI
- No exclamation marks in alerts
- Avoid buzzwords

### Example

❌ *“⚠️ URGENT! Your document is about to expire!!!”*  
✅ *“Your passport expires in 30 days.”*

---

## 7. Messaging Pillars

### 7.1 Peace of Mind
“Nothing expires without you knowing.”

### 7.2 Quiet Automation
“Works in the background.”

### 7.3 Control & Ownership
“You decide. You confirm. You own the data.”

### 7.4 Professional Trust
Built for serious people, used daily without friction.

---

## 8. Visual Identity (Guidelines)

### 8.1 Color Palette (Conceptual)
- Primary: Neutral dark (charcoal / deep blue)
- Secondary: Soft gray
- Accent: Muted amber or blue (used sparingly for alerts)

No bright reds by default.

### 8.2 Typography
- Sans-serif
- High readability
- No decorative fonts

### 8.3 Layout
- White space is a feature
- Few elements per screen
- Clear hierarchy

---

## 9. Logo Usage (Principles)

- Simple
- Flat
- Works in monochrome
- Avoid gradients in v1

Logo should feel timeless, not trendy.

---

## 10. UX Principles

### 10.1 Silent by Default
No action required unless necessary.

### 10.2 Confirm Before Commit
Users always confirm detected expirations.

### 10.3 Predictable Behavior
Same rules, same outcomes.

### 10.4 No Dark Patterns
No hidden settings, no forced upgrades.

---

## 11. Email & Notification Style

### Subject Lines
- Informational
- Neutral
- Date-driven

Example:
- “Upcoming expiration: March 12”

### Email Body
- What is expiring
- When
- What (if anything) to do

Nothing else.

---

## 12. Pricing & Brand Alignment

Price should feel:
- Fair
- Predictable
- Justified by peace of mind

No aggressive discounts. No fake urgency.

---

## 13. Brand Do’s and Don’ts

### Do
- Be precise
- Be calm
- Be boring in a good way

### Don’t
- Use fear
- Use hype
- Over-communicate

---

## 14. Brand Promise

Expiration Guardian promises:

> If something important has a date, you will know — calmly, clearly, and on time.

Nothing more. Nothing less.

---

## 15. Internal Brand Test

Before shipping any copy, feature, or campaign, ask:

> Does this reduce or increase user anxiety?

If it increases anxiety, it does not ship.
